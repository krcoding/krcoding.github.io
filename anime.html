An Introduction

Anime and Manga. For a lot of people, those terms draw a blank. Or, at least, a vague notion of Pokemon. Maybe of people with blue hair and inhumanly large eyes. Hey, that's fine. No judgement here; Pokemon was my first anime, and that's pretty on the mark. (Okay, actually, redaction: My first anime was Cardcaptor Sakura, but I digress.) Still, for the uninitiated: Anime (アニメ) is animation produced in Japan, oftentimes in Japanese. Manga (漫画) are comics originally written in Japanese.

Notions of Genre in Anime/Manga

Anime and manga-specific genres tend to intersect more with ideas of audience than with their content. So, for example, two of the most popular genres of manga and anime are shounen (少年), which are for younger men, and shoujo (少女), which are intended for a younger, female audience. Each genre has commonly-accepted tropes and "hallmarks", though series in this umbrella by no means have to follow this mold.

A screencap of the cult-hit murder mystery visual novel "Danganronpa 2: Goodbye Despair". (Source: Gamer Compatible)

So what's a Visual Novel? How does it relate?

Visual Novels are a more niche genre of video games with a cult following both in Japan and around the world. These games play like a choose-your-own-adventure novel, but are more interactive. Oftentimes, they have a mystery or romance element. Some more famous visual novels (which I am examining in my IS) include the Danganronpa franchise, Hatoful Boyfriend, and STEINS;GATE. My IS will mainly focus on Danganronpa 2: Goodbye Despair (Pictured above) and an American visual novel, Analogue: A Hate Story.
