Notions of Genre & Narrative

Because video games have those too (contrary to popular belief.)

A screencap from Toby Fox's smash-hit RPG Undertale. Toriel, a friendly guide, helps the player character through a puzzle. (Image Credit: Hardcore Gaming 101).

People usually don't connect "narrative" with "video games", but I found out fairly quickly that that is incorrect! Every single game I've ever played has had a narrative of some sort, whether it's "Candy Crush" or "Final Fantasy". No matter what, games--and pretty much all media that humans produce--has to have a narrative. But, why's that?

Why Narratives?

To be honest, there's a lot of reasons. But, the main reason I've found so far is that narratives mirror the structure of our lives, giving us a reason to care about them. Everything we do has a narrative... At least, that's what I'm arguing in my IS!

That got a bit philosophical there. But hey, you get my drift.

What's up with Genre? Tropes?

Genre gives us a way to classify narratives, and tropes are different conventions within genre, and fiction as a whole. (If you want to learn more about the nitty-gritty of both, along with narrative, check out my sidebars!)

Understanding genre not only gives viewers (or in my case, players) an idea of what to expect walking in. For example, you wouldn't walk into a romantic comedy expecting realistic romances. You watch them so you can cheer at the couple finally getting together! You wouldn't walk into a first-person shooter game (like Call of Duty) expecting anime character designs. It's that kind of thing.
